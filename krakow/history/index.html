Kraków - history


Note!

This page is still work in progress.


History: (source)

  • First documented mentioning of Kraków was an account by the Cordoban merchant Ibrahim ibn Jacob from 965.
  • During the ruling of Kazimierz Odnowiciel (1034-1058) Kraków became the capital of Poland, due to the damage of Gniezno made by a raid of Bretislav I in 1038.
  • Kraków got destroyed during the Mongolian raid in 1241.
  • Kraków received the staple right in 1306.
  • From 1596, the process of moving the royal court of Zygmunt III Waza from Kraków to Warszawa began, ending around 1611.
  • Kraków was the formal capital of Poland until the last parition of Poland in 1795.
  • After the Third Partition of Poland in 1795, Kraków was occupied by the Austrians.
  • There was a major fire in Kraków in 1850, which caused 10% of the city to get destroyed.
  • German troops occupied the city six days after the start of the Second World War.
  • During the German occupation (1939-1945), Kraków functioned as the capital of the General Government.
  • The post-war period saw strong territorial and population development of the city.
  • From 1st of January, 1957 to 31st of May, 1975, the city of Kraków was a separate autonomous voivodeship.
  • In 2023, the 3rd European Games took place in Kraków.
